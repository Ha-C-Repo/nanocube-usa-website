What a complete shell includes

Structure and envelope.

Structure
  • Conventional steel frame: W-shapes, HSS, channels, angles
  • Steel roof framing and open-web joists
  • Steel roof deck or standing-seam roof system
  • Opening framing, lintels, and lateral bracing
  • Miscellaneous metals: stairs, rails, supports
  • Equipment support platforms
  • Anchor rods and base plates
Envelope
  • Insulated metal panels (IMPs), roof and walls
  • Exterior aluminum panel systems
  • Continuous insulation and vapor control
  • Waterproofing and flashings
  • Soffits
  • Gutters and downspouts
  • Wall girts, wind columns, eave and base supports
What we build in steel

The steel vocabulary.

The expressed-steel look is conventional rolled steel, fabricated and erected as a finished, exposed structure. The vocabulary below is the language we build in.

Exposed gable moment frame

Clear-span steel and full glass, with clerestory daylight.

Open canopy, rod-braced

Exposed wide-flange beams on columns, round tension-rod bracing.

Exterior braced-frame tower

Painted structural steel as the facade. Expressed X-bracing.

Mid-rise braced steel frame

Expressed lateral system over a glass curtainwall.

Sculptural exterior stair

Painted steel egress turned signature, with a faceted screen.

Metal-clad envelope and canopy

Standing-seam panel skin on a steel frame, exposed entry canopy.

The steel behind it: wide-flange columns and beams, HSS posts and braces, round tension-rod bracing, open-web joists and metal deck, channels, angles, bent plate, and standing-seam and panel envelopes. All conventional rolled steel, fabricated and erected as a finished, exposed structure.
